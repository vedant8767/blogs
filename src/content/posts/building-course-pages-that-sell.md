Selling an online course is different from selling software. Your landing page needs to educate, inspire, and build trust — all before asking for the sale. Here's how to do it with the Chaipress course template.

## The Course Page Formula

Our **Course Sales** template arranges sections in a proven order:

1. **Hero (Centered)** — Course title, subtitle, enrollment CTA
2. **Stats** — Social proof numbers (students enrolled, rating, completion rate)
3. **Features Grid** — What students will learn
4. **Curriculum** — Module-by-module breakdown
5. **Instructor** — Bio, photo, credentials
6. **Testimonials** — Student success stories
7. **Pricing** — Plan options (self-paced vs. premium)
8. **FAQ** — Common objections answered
9. **CTA** — Final enrollment push

Each section addresses a specific question in the buyer's mind.

## Section-by-Section Guide

### The Hero: Set Expectations

Your course hero should answer three questions instantly:

- **What is this?** → Course title
- **Who is it for?** → Subtitle describing the target student
- **What's the outcome?** → Specific, measurable result

```
Good: "Master Product Management in 8 Weeks"
Better: "Go From Beginner to Confident PM —
         Land Your First Product Role in 8 Weeks"
```

Use the **badge** field for urgency: "Enrollment Open", "Limited Spots", "New Cohort Starting April 1".

### Stats: Prove Demand

Numbers build instant credibility. Effective stats for courses:

- Students enrolled (show momentum)
- Average rating (show quality)
- Completion rate (show engagement)
- Program length (set expectations)

### Curriculum: Show the Roadmap

The curriculum section uses an **accordion layout** — each module expands to reveal lessons. Tips:

- **Name modules clearly** — "Module 3: User Research" not "Module 3"
- **Include lesson duration** — Helps students plan their time
- **Show enough detail** — 3-5 lessons per module is ideal
- **Open the first module** — Give a preview of what's inside

### Instructor: Build Authority

Your instructor section should include:

- Professional photo (not casual)
- Current role + relevant title
- Short bio (2-3 sentences)
- 3-4 credential bullet points
- Optional social links

**Pro tip:** Mention specific companies, numbers, or achievements. "Helped 200+ companies launch products" is stronger than "experienced product manager."

### Pricing: Make It Simple

For courses, we recommend **2-3 tiers**:

| Tier | Includes | Price Point |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Self-Paced | Video access + community | $$ |
| Premium | + mentoring + certificate | $$$ |
| Team | Bulk pricing + group features | Custom |

Highlight the premium tier as "Most Popular" — the anchoring effect makes it feel like the best value.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too much text** — Let the curriculum section handle details
2. **No social proof** — Even 3 testimonials make a huge difference
3. **Vague outcomes** — "Learn product management" vs. "Land your first PM role"
4. **No urgency** — Add a cohort deadline or early-bird pricing
5. **Hiding the price** — Transparency builds trust
